Grants

Quantifying the potential contribution of asymptomatic screening and treatment to malaria control and elimination

NIH National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Disease (Project # 1F31AI172387-01)

In regions where malaria transmission is high, chronic asymptomatic malaria infections are common and are major contributors to continued transmission of malaria. A recent field trial showed that asymptomatic screening, testing, and treatment (STT) interventions could reduce transmission from humans to mosquitoes. In this study, I will use agent-based modeling to evaluate the potential impact and cost- effectiveness of STT on long-term malaria control and elimination across a diverse set of epidemiological settings.
